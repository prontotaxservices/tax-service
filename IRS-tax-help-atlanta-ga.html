Second, it is not the same as tax planning. Tax planning is forward-looking and strategic, whereas IRS tax help is usually problem-centered or process-centered around current or past obligations.

It also differs from bookkeeping. Bookkeeping concerns the recording and categorization of transactions over time, while IRS tax help generally begins when the taxpayer needs to interpret or act on an IRS-related requirement. Another distinction is with tax resolution. Resolution work tends to involve more formal dispute, collection, or enforcement contexts, whereas “IRS tax help” in common local-market language may include early-stage questions that have not escalated into formal resolution. Finally, it differs from legal representation. Not all IRS-related help involves legal advocacy, and the phrase should not automatically imply courtroom, litigation, or attorney-led work.
